Project Title: IS/IT Cybersecurity Job Hunt

This semester-long project will focus on job hunting to prepare and apply to internships/part-time opportunities in relation to my studies. This will involve me conducting a self-assessment of all my academic achievements, creating and tailoring application documents based on job requirements, communicating with professors and peers to increase confidence in applying to positions, and getting feedback on resumes and cover letters from stakeholders (professors, counselors, peers, etc.). This project will implement the cybersecurity knowledge I've learned thus far and bring me closer to my mission of finding working opportunities.

Project Goal: The goal of this project is to secure meaningful progress toward a cybersecurity/information systems internship by preparing professional application materials, submitting at least 5 targeted applications, and developing a networking pipeline that leads to recruiter or interview opportunities.

📈 Success Metrics:

  • Apply to 5-10 positions by semester end
  • Achieve 80% stakeholder approval on resume drafts
  • Expand LinkedIn network by 5+ professional connections
  • Complete ISC2 CC study plan with 85% practice exam scores

🧰 9. Project Tools

▼
Instructions: Explain your tool selection, why it fits the chosen methodology, and include screenshots showing how you set up the project.

Tool Selection Explanation

I looked at three tools for my project: Microsoft Project, Trello, and Monday.com.

Trello is great for lightweight Agile boards, but I needed both a timeline and a board. Because my project is Hybrid, I chose Monday.com: Gantt/Timeline views cover the predictive milestones (resume package, tracker, interview kit) while a Kanban board handles the iterative weekly application/networking cycle.

I ended up going with Monday.com. Honestly, when I first opened it, I felt overwhelmed too, but once I learned how to use it, I realized it was exactly what I needed. Monday.com let me group tasks by deliverables, set dependencies, and assign timelines. For example, I could clearly show that applying to jobs depends on finishing resumes, which depends on resume prep. It took me a couple of days to set everything up, but now I have a timeline that matches my WBS and methodology diagram (and the class schedule (PMI Process Group)). It helps me stay organized and keeps me from losing track of where I'm at during the semester.
